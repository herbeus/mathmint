Questions parents & teachers ask

What is skip counting?

Skip counting is counting up in equal steps — by 2s, 5s or 10s — instead of one at a time. It builds number sense and is the bridge from counting to multiplication.

Why does skip counting matter?

Counting by 2s, 5s and 10s underpins the 2×, 5× and 10× times tables, place value, telling time, and counting money. It's one of the highest-leverage skills in early math.

What order should kids learn skip counting in?

Most kids start with counting by 10s and 5s (clear, satisfying patterns), then 2s. Each worksheet above gives a few anchor numbers and leaves the rest blank to fill in.
