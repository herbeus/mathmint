Questions parents & teachers ask

What is skip counting by 5s?

Skip counting by 5s means counting up in steps of 5 — 5, 10, 15, 20… instead of one at a time. Counting by 5s lands on a number ending in 5 or 0 every time. It's the pattern behind reading a clock, counting nickels, and tally marks — so it pays off everywhere.

What grade is counting by 5s for?

Counting by 5s is usually taught in Kindergarten–Grade 2. These worksheets keep the sequences short and give a few numbers as anchors, so they suit early learners building number sense.
