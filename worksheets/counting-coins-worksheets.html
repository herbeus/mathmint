Questions parents & teachers ask

How do I teach counting coins?

Learn the quarter as 25¢ — two quarters make 50¢, four make a dollar. Counting a handful is easiest from the biggest coin down: quarters, dimes, nickels, then pennies. Print a fresh sheet, count a few together each day, and check with the answer key — short, regular practice beats one long session.

What grade is counting coins money for?

This is usually taught in Grade 2. Now all four coins are in play: penny 1¢, nickel 5¢, dime 10¢, quarter 25¢. Start from the quarters and work down to the pennies, keeping a running total as you go.

What are the coins worth?

A penny is 1¢, a nickel is 5¢, a dime is 10¢ and a quarter is 25¢. A dime is the smallest coin but worth more than a nickel — value isn't about size. To total a handful, count from the most valuable coin down and keep a running total.
