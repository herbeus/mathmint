Questions parents & teachers ask

How do I teach bills & coins?

Separate bills from coins. Count the bills for the dollar amount, count the coins for the cents, then join them with a decimal point: $6 and 35¢ is $6.35. Print a fresh sheet, count a few together each day, and check with the answer key — short, regular practice beats one long session.

What grade is bills & coins money for?

This is usually taught in Grade 2 & Grade 3. Put $1 and $5 bills together with coins and the total runs past a dollar. Count the bills first for the whole dollars, then add the coins as cents, and write the amount as dollars-and-cents like $6.35.

What are the coins worth?

A penny is 1¢, a nickel is 5¢, a dime is 10¢ and a quarter is 25¢. A dime is the smallest coin but worth more than a nickel — value isn't about size. To total a handful, count from the most valuable coin down and keep a running total.
