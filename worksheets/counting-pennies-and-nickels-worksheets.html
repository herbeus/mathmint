Questions parents & teachers ask

How do I teach pennies & nickels?

Sort the coins into two piles first: nickels and pennies. Count the nickels by 5s, then count on the pennies by 1s. Counting the bigger coins first keeps the running total simple. Print a fresh sheet, count a few together each day, and check with the answer key — short, regular practice beats one long session.

What grade is pennies & nickels money for?

This is usually taught in Kindergarten & Grade 1. A penny is worth 1¢ and a nickel is worth 5¢. To total a small handful, count the nickels first by 5s — 5, 10, 15 — then count on the pennies one at a time.

What are the coins worth?

A penny is 1¢, a nickel is 5¢, a dime is 10¢ and a quarter is 25¢. A dime is the smallest coin but worth more than a nickel — value isn't about size. To total a handful, count from the most valuable coin down and keep a running total.
