Questions parents & teachers ask

What is a number bond?

A number bond is a picture of part-part-whole: a 'whole' number on top joined to its two 'parts' below. It shows at a glance that the parts combine to make the whole — the foundation of addition and subtraction.

Why do number bonds matter?

Number bonds build the flexible number sense kids need to add and subtract fluently. Knowing the pairs that make 10 especially — the make-ten facts — speeds up almost all later mental math and place-value work.

What order should kids learn number bonds in?

Most kids start with bonds to 5, then the all-important bonds to 10, then bonds to 20. Each worksheet above leaves one value blank — a part or the whole — so children practise in both directions.
