Questions parents & teachers ask

In what order should kids learn the times tables?

A common path: 2×, 10× and 5× first (they have clear patterns), then 3×, 4× and 11×, then the trickier 6×, 7×, 8×, 9× and 12×. Each table above has a memory tip to make it click.

How long does it take to learn the times tables?

With five minutes of focused practice a day, most kids get solid across all tables in a few months. Short, regular sessions with a fresh worksheet beat occasional long ones.
